Akua Adjei, Esq.


Akua, the founder of the firm, obtained an LLB with Advanced French (Hons) from the University of Kent at Canterbury in the U.K, a PGDip in Legal Practice from the College of Law in London, and a PGCE (Modern Foreign Languages – French) from Middlesex University in London. She is a solicitor of the Supreme Court of England and Wales (non-practising), and a solicitor and barrister of the Supreme Court of Ghana, with 17 years’ collective experience of legal practice in both jurisdictions.


Akua provides, inter alia, general corporate advice and legal counsel on negotiating, structuring and closing joint ventures and mergers & acquisitions, corporate restructurings, obtaining regulatory approvals, commercial contracts, and technology transfer agreements. She advises companies in the energy, oil and gas, banking and insurance sectors.


She has extensive experience in employment law, corporate law, and personal and corporate immigration law and practice, as well as the incorporation of companies in Ghana, conducting due diligence, registration with the Ghana Investment Promotion Centre (GIPC), the Ghana Free Zones Authority (GFZA), and sector-specific institutions such as the National Petroleum Authority (NPA), the Petroleum Commission, and the Energy Commission.


Akua also has wide experience of providing company secretarial, corporate governance and compliance services to local and international companies in the telecommunications, oil and gas, banking and insurance sectors, assists in maintaining the corporate registers of companies, and provides compliance advisory support to the boards of directors and management of several local and international companies in conformity with local laws and international best practices.


She serves as a director on the boards of a number of companies, is fluent in written and spoken English, and has practical knowledge of French.

Crystal Quarshie, Paralegal



Crystal Quarshie holds an LLB (Hons) from the University of Sussex in the UK. As an undergraduate student, she served as an international student representative of the university.

Prior to joining Rhema Law as a paralegal, she worked as an administrator and immigration law assistant for a law firm in London, UK, and interned with a number of law firms in Accra, Ghana, during which she harnessed her research and administrative skills.


As a paralegal at Rhema Law, she assists the legal team with legal research, and liaises with various government agencies on clients’ matters as required. She also provides general administrative assistance to the legal team.


Crystal has practical knowledge of French and is proficient in social media management.
